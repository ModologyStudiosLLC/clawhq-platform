How to Deploy a Complete AI Team in Under 5 Minutes

The traditional path to deploying a multi-agent AI team takes weeks and costs engineering time you don't have. Here's how to do it in 5 minutes — 30+ agents, all channels wired, one command.

The promise of AI teams has long been hindered by setup complexity. Organizations spend weeks — sometimes months — wiring together multiple AI agents, integrating them with communication channels, and ensuring they work cohesively. Manual integration of frameworks, custom configuration for each agent type, complex security setup, time-intensive testing. Most teams underestimate how much hidden infrastructure work exists between "we have one agent" and "we have a working AI team."

What "Deploy in 5 Minutes" Actually Means

ClawHQ is a pre-integrated platform that ships with 30+ agents already wired to all major channels. There's no framework to assemble, no channel integrations to build, no state management layer to design. You configure what you need and run one command. Here's the actual process:

1

Pull the image

Pull the ClawHQ Docker image. No additional dependencies required — everything is bundled.

~1 minute
2

Set environment variables

Configure your channel credentials (Discord bot token, Slack app token, etc.) using the pre-built template. One .env file.

~1 minute
3

Deploy

One command launches your entire AI team.

docker compose up
~1 minute
4

Configure agents

Access the web dashboard to enable the agents you want and set their parameters. Disable the ones you don't need.

~1 minute
5

Go live

Your AI team is operational. 30+ agents ready across all configured channels.

~1 minute

What You Get Out of the Box

ClawHQ doesn't ship a framework — it ships a working product. Every agent has:

  • Channel integrations pre-wired: Discord, Slack, Telegram, and webhooks — no per-channel engineering work
  • Persistent memory: 200K token context window with cross-session learning, shared across your team
  • Security and audit trails: Built into the Rust-based autonomous agent OS, not bolted on after
  • Zero-downtime updates: Platform updates ship without interrupting running agents

Agents in the default stack include: Researcher, Email Assistant, Doc Writer, Coder, Analyst, Compliance Auditor, Security Scanner, Market Researcher, Content Creator, Legal Assistant, and more.

A financial services company needed agents for customer onboarding, compliance monitoring, market research, content generation, and security auditing. Their requirements were non-trivial: multi-channel, enterprise security, audit trail compliance.
