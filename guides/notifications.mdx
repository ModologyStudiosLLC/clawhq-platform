ClawHQ can send alerts to Slack when your agents go offline or when AI spending approaches your budget limit.

## Setting up Slack notifications

1. Create an [Incoming Webhook](https://api.slack.com/messaging/webhooks) in your Slack workspace
2. Go to **Settings → Notifications** in the dashboard
3. Paste the webhook URL and click **Test** to verify delivery
4. Enable the alert types you want

## Budget alerts

When enabled, ClawHQ checks your current spending against the threshold set in **Settings → Budget**. If spending reaches or exceeds that percentage of your monthly budget, a Slack message is sent:

> *ClawHQ Budget Alert* — spending has reached 85% of monthly budget ($17.00 / $20.00, threshold: 80%).

Budget alerts are checked whenever settings are saved. For continuous monitoring, configure your deployment to poll `/api/settings` on a schedule, or trigger checks from a cron job.

## Health alerts

When enabled, ClawHQ sends a Slack alert when a required service (OpenClaw, Paperclip, OpenFang) has consecutive health check failures.

**Failure threshold** — default is 3 consecutive failures. Set this to 1 for immediate alerts, or higher (5–10) to reduce noise for flaky connections.

**Cooldown** — after alerting, ClawHQ waits 30 minutes before alerting again for the same service. This prevents alert floods during extended outages.

## Configuration

| Setting | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Slack webhook URL | Incoming webhook from your Slack app |
| Budget alert | Notify when spend hits threshold % |
| Health alert | Notify when a required service goes offline |
| Failure threshold | Consecutive failures before alerting (1–10) |

## Notification payload

Alerts are sent as plain Slack messages. No attachments or Block Kit — just a text string that works in any Slack channel configuration.
