## Overview

Competitor Intel produces evidence-based competitive teardowns — every finding tied to a specific, observable signal. It uses Helmer's 7 Powers framework to score moats, map vulnerabilities, and generate actionable attack briefs. It also runs quarterly re-audits and diffs against your stored baseline.

## Teardown structure

A full analysis produces 7 sections:

### 1. Positioning Snapshot
- ICP (ideal customer profile)
- Headline positioning and core value claim
- Tone and persona
- Pricing tier (budget / mid-market / enterprise / usage-based)
- Primary channel (PLG / sales-led / community / content)

### 2. Offer & Pricing Analysis
- What they actually sell (not just website copy)
- Pricing structure and public tiers
- Conspicuous gaps in the offer
- Free tier / trial strategy

### 3. Content & Channel Fingerprint
- Primary acquisition channels (SEO, paid, community, partnerships)
- Content themes and cadence
- Social presence and tone
- Where customers talk about them (Reddit, G2, Twitter, Slack groups)

### 4. Moat Assessment (7 Powers)

Each power scored **Strong / Weak / None** with evidence:

| Power | Question |
|-------|----------|
| Scale Economies | Does cost per unit fall as volume grows? |
| Network Effects | Does the product get more valuable as more users join? |
| Counter-Positioning | Do they do something incumbents can't copy without cannibalization? |
| Switching Costs | How painful is it for customers to leave? |
| Branding | Do they command a premium from perceived quality or identity? |
| Cornered Resource | Do they have exclusive access to a key asset (IP, talent, data, geography)? |
| Process Power | Do they have a proprietary process hard to replicate? |

### 5. Vulnerability Map
Three specific competitive gaps with supporting evidence, framed as exploits:

> "They can't do X because Y, which means Z is an opening."

### 6. Attack Brief
- Immediate tactical moves (executable in <30 days)
- Medium-term positioning shifts (30–90 days)
- One counter-positioning angle that would be painful for them to copy

### 7. Re-Audit Protocol
- 3 signals to watch that indicate a strategy shift
- Recommended re-audit cadence (monthly / quarterly)

## Evidence standards

All claims are labeled:
- **HIGH** — direct evidence (website copy, pricing page, job posting, tweet)
- **MEDIUM** — strong inference
- **LOW** — speculation (quarantined in a separate section, never presented as conclusions)

No teardown is produced without actually reading the competitor's website.

## Quarterly re-audit

The agent runs automatically on the **first of every quarter at 9am ET**. It compares the new teardown to the stored baseline and outputs a DIFF section:

- Positioning or pricing changes
- Powers gained or lost
- Vulnerability map shifts
- Strategy signals that fired
